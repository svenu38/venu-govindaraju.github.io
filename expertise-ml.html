Applied Machine Learning

Designing practical ML systems, not just models in notebooks

My machine learning experience sits at the intersection of research and production: I work with real, messy data; build models that solve concrete problems; and integrate them into data platforms so that non-technical stakeholders can actually use the results.

What I’m strong at in ML

End-to-End ML Pipelines

From problem framing and data understanding to model deployment: data cleaning, feature engineering, training, hyperparameter tuning, evaluation, and handover to production teams.

Deep Learning for Real Data

Experience with CNNs and LSTMs for sequence modeling, attention-based TCNs for RUL prediction, and multimodal architectures combining audio and video (e.g., ResNet3D + ECAPA-TDNN).

Structured & Time-Series Data

Built models for energy efficiency, heart failure prediction, and process activity forecasting, using classical ML and deep learning on time-series and tabular data.

Evaluation & Robustness

Beyond accuracy: calibration, error analysis, cross-validation, and robustness checks. Familiar with metrics like EER, ROC curves, confusion matrices, and challenge-specific protocols.

Standard ML Workflow I Apply Across Projects

Whether I’m working on biometrics, sensor data, healthcare, or business processes, I follow a consistent workflow to keep projects structured, reproducible, and easy to hand over to teams.

1

Problem Definition & Success Criteria

Clarify the business or research question, define the prediction target, constraints (latency, interpretability, data availability), and agree on KPIs (AUC, F1, EER, RMSE, etc.).

Requirements & KPI design
2

Data Understanding & Cleaning

Explore datasets, handle missing values and outliers, check data leakage, understand temporal aspects, and create clear training/validation/test splits that respect reality.

EDA & quality checks
3

Feature Engineering & Baseline Models

Build a solid baseline using classical models (logistic regression, random forest, XGBoost) with good features, to establish a realistic performance floor before moving to deep learning.

Baselines & iterations
4

Deep Learning & Architecture Design

Where it adds value, design and train neural architectures (CNN/LSTM/TCN, multimodal fusion), run controlled experiments, and track results with experiment tracking tools.

Advanced modeling
5

Evaluation, Stress Testing & Interpretation

Compare models using held-out sets, cross-validation, and challenge protocols; perform error analysis, analyze failure modes, and translate results into clear insights for stakeholders.

Model selection
6

Packaging & Integration

Package the final model (Python package, REST service, batch job) and integrate it with data pipelines on Azure/Databricks, ensuring it’s ready for deployment and monitoring.

Deployment-ready

Selected ML Projects

Multimodal Lip–Audio Authentication

Built a dual-stream system combining ResNet3D (lip motion) and ECAPA-TDNN (speech) with a Gated Multimodal Unit for fusion. Optimized using triplet loss + BCE for open-set identity verification on a lip–audio biometric dataset.

Business Process Activity Prediction

Used CNN + LSTM architectures on event logs to predict next activities in business processes, improving predictive accuracy compared to classical baselines and supporting proactive monitoring.

Heart Failure Risk Prediction

Built classification models on clinical tabular data to identify high-risk patients. Focused on feature importance, class imbalance handling, and interpretable metrics for stakeholders.

RUL Prediction with Attention-based TCN

Applied temporal convolutional networks with attention mechanisms for Remaining Useful Life prediction on industrial time-series data, capturing temporal dependencies and degradation patterns.
