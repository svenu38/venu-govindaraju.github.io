Conference · Springer Multimodal Biometric Authentication Using Lip Motion and Spoken Passphrases

V. Siddapura Govindaraju, et al.

Proceedings of the International Conference on Image Analysis and Processing (ICIAP), Springer, 2026

DOI: 10.1007/978-3-032-11381-8_45

Abstract — This work presents a dual-factor biometric authentication system developed for the BIOVID Challenge 2025, targeting open-set verification scenarios using synchronized audio-visual data. The system employs a dual-stream deep learning architecture combining a ResNet3D-18 network with a Bidirectional GRU for spatiotemporal lip-motion modeling and a fine-tuned ECAPA-TDNN for robust speech embedding extraction.

A Gated Multimodal Unit (GMU) fuses modality-specific representations into a 256-dimensional joint embedding used for both classification and embedding-based identity verification. Training uses a composite loss combining triplet loss with semi-hard negative mining and cross-entropy loss, enabling robust open-set decision making.

Keywords: Biometric authentication · Audiovisual fusion · Open-set recognition · ECAPA-TDNN · Lip reading · GMU fusion · Triplet loss

Ongoing Research · LivDet 2025 Challenge Adversarial Liveness Detector (ALD): A Progressive Deep Learning Framework for Fingerprint Presentation Attack Detection

Carlo Sansone, Stefano Marrone, Venu Siddapura Govindaraju

University of Naples Federico II — Ongoing (2025)

Challenge Website: https://sites.unica.it/livdet/

Abstract — This ongoing research presents a robust fingerprint liveness detection and user verification system developed for participation in the LivDet 2025 Challenge. The proposed solution integrates a deep convolutional neural network trained on both clean and adversarially augmented fingerprint images with a handcrafted feature matcher to compute an Integrated Match Score (IMS), reflecting both liveness and identity confidence.

The framework follows a three-phase progressive training strategy: (1) training on clean images with traditional augmentations (ALD_A), (2) incorporation of adversarial samples generated using a modified DeepFool attack (ALD_B), and (3) retraining on a combined clean and adversarial dataset without traditional augmentations (ALD_C). The final model is based on EfficientNet-B6 and outputs a normalized liveness score.

The liveness score is combined with a SIFT-based fingerprint matcher using a decision rule inspired by prior literature to generate the Integrated Match Score. Experimental evaluation is currently ongoing on official LivDet 2025 datasets to assess robustness across multiple fingerprint sensors and real-world presentation attack scenarios.

Status: Ongoing — Results and publication forthcoming
